Medical Chronology Software Compared: 2026 Buyer’s Guide

February 20, 2026 · 12 min read

The market for AI-powered medical chronology and record summarization software has matured significantly since 2024. What was once a two-player race between EvenUp and a handful of manual review services has expanded into a crowded field of more than a dozen platforms, each with distinct strengths, limitations, and pricing structures.

This guide evaluates the ten most prominent tools available in 2026, with a focus on what matters most to personal injury law firms: accuracy, turnaround time, cost, data privacy, and the breadth of features beyond basic summarization.

The 10 Platforms at a Glance

1. EvenUp MedChrons

Cloud-based · $300–$800+ per case · 24–48 hour turnaround

EvenUp remains the market leader by revenue and name recognition. Their platform combines AI processing with human review to produce medical chronologies and demand packages. The quality is generally strong, particularly for standard soft-tissue PI cases. However, pricing has increased substantially since their launch, with many firms reporting costs at the upper end of the range for complex cases.

Strengths

  • High-quality output with human review
  • Large training dataset (250K+ cases)
  • Established workflow integrations

Weaknesses

  • Expensive at scale
  • Cloud-only (PHI uploaded)
  • Limited customization options

2. Supio

Cloud-based · Monthly subscription + $50–$150/case · Minutes to hours

Supio offers a comprehensive AI-first platform with medical chronologies, case economics analysis, and demand letter support. Their pricing is more moderate than EvenUp, making them a popular mid-market option. The platform includes useful search and Q&A features for querying medical records conversationally.

Strengths

  • Strong natural language search
  • Case economics module
  • More affordable than EvenUp

Weaknesses

  • Per-case fees still add up
  • Cloud-only processing
  • Quality varies on complex cases

3. Wisedocs

Cloud-based · $0.10–$0.35/page · Minutes

Wisedocs focuses primarily on document processing and organization rather than full narrative chronologies. They excel at deduplication, page classification, and OCR. Useful as a preprocessing step, but most firms need additional tools for the actual chronology and analysis work.

Strengths

  • Excellent deduplication engine
  • Fast processing speeds
  • Good for high-volume preprocessing

Weaknesses

  • Does not generate full chronologies
  • Limited analysis capabilities
  • Per-page pricing on large records

4. InPractice AI

Cloud-based · $0.05/page · Minutes

InPractice offers some of the lowest per-page pricing in the market. The platform provides medical record summaries, chronologies, and basic analysis. Output quality is adequate for straightforward cases but may lack the depth and nuance that complex litigation demands.

Strengths

  • Very low per-page cost
  • Simple, intuitive interface
  • Good for budget-conscious firms

Weaknesses

  • Basic analysis capabilities
  • Less accurate on complex records
  • Limited feature set

5. Dodonai

Cloud-based · ~$30/month subscription · Minutes

Dodonai targets the budget end of the market with a low monthly subscription fee. The platform provides AI-generated medical summaries and basic chronological organization. A good entry point for firms testing AI-assisted summarization for the first time, though it lacks the depth of more mature platforms.

Strengths

  • Very affordable monthly fee
  • No per-case charges
  • Easy to get started

Weaknesses

  • Limited feature depth
  • Smaller development team
  • Less polished output format

6. Filevine MedChron

Cloud-based · Bundled with Filevine CMS · Varies

Filevine's medical chronology feature is integrated into their broader case management platform. If your firm already uses Filevine, this is a convenient native option. However, it requires being fully invested in the Filevine ecosystem, and the AI capabilities are less specialized than dedicated medical chronology tools.

Strengths

  • Native CMS integration
  • Seamless workflow if on Filevine
  • No separate vendor to manage

Weaknesses

  • Requires Filevine subscription
  • Less specialized AI engine
  • Limited for non-Filevine firms

7. Superinsight

Cloud-based · Custom pricing · Hours

Superinsight positions itself as an enterprise-grade medical record analysis platform. The tool provides structured summaries, timeline views, and search functionality. Pricing is typically negotiated and tends to favor larger firms with higher volumes.

Strengths

  • Enterprise-grade features
  • Strong structured data extraction
  • Good for large firm deployments

Weaknesses

  • Opaque pricing
  • May be over-engineered for small firms
  • Cloud-only architecture

8. Legalyze

Cloud-based · ~$150/month · Minutes to hours

Legalyze provides AI-generated medical summaries with a focus on simplicity and affordability. The monthly subscription model with no per-case fees makes it predictable to budget. Good for firms handling moderate case volumes who want consistent monthly costs.

Strengths

  • Flat monthly pricing
  • No per-case surprise costs
  • Clean user interface

Weaknesses

  • Moderate feature set
  • Cloud processing only
  • Less effective on large record sets

9. CaseFleet

Cloud-based · $30+/month per user · Varies

CaseFleet is primarily a timeline and fact management tool that has added AI-powered medical record processing. Its strength lies in the ability to build detailed case timelines with linked evidence. Less focused on medical chronology specifically, but useful as a broader case organization tool.

Strengths

  • Excellent timeline visualization
  • Strong fact-linking capabilities
  • Good for trial preparation

Weaknesses

  • Not a dedicated medical chronology tool
  • Per-user pricing adds up
  • Steeper learning curve

10. MedRecords AI

On-premise · One-time perpetual license · Minutes

MedRecords AI takes a fundamentally different architectural approach. The software runs entirely on the firm's own hardware. There are no per-case fees, no monthly subscriptions, and no PHI uploaded to external servers. The feature set is among the broadest in the market, including medical chronology, demand package generation, negligence detection, case valuation, injury visualization, and automated task management.

Strengths

  • No recurring costs
  • On-premise data privacy
  • Negligence detection (unique)
  • Full feature suite included

Weaknesses

  • Requires local hardware
  • Initial setup more involved than cloud
  • Newer to market

How to Choose the Right Tool

The "best" medical chronology software depends entirely on your firm's circumstances. Here are the key decision factors:

If Cost Is Your Primary Concern

For firms where budget drives the decision, the perpetual license model of MedRecords AI offers the lowest long-term cost of ownership. Among cloud options, Dodonai and InPractice AI offer the lowest per-unit pricing, though with reduced feature sets.

If Data Privacy Is Non-Negotiable

MedRecords AI is the only platform in this comparison that processes data entirely on-premise. Every other tool requires uploading PHI to external cloud servers. For firms handling sensitive cases or operating under heightened confidentiality requirements, this is a decisive differentiator.

If You Need the Broadest Feature Set

EvenUp, Supio, and MedRecords AI offer the most comprehensive feature suites. However, MedRecords AI is the only platform offering negligence detection and injury visualization, features that no cloud competitor currently provides.

If You Want Minimal Setup

Cloud-based platforms like Supio, Legalyze, and Dodonai require no installation and can be operational within minutes. This is their fundamental convenience advantage over on-premise solutions.

The Bottom Line

The medical chronology software market is more competitive than ever, which benefits buyers. Firms that were previously limited to expensive per-case vendors now have options at every price point. The key is to match your firm's volume, budget, privacy requirements, and feature needs against the specific strengths of each platform.

Whatever you choose, the days of manually reviewing thousands of pages of medical records are over. The question is no longer whether to adopt AI-assisted summarization, but which tool best fits your practice.
